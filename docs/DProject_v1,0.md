# **D-PROJECT ARCHITECTURE v1.0**

### *A Public Governance Framework for Fair AI Surplus Distribution*

---

## **0. Purpose**

The D-Project is a public governance architecture ensuring that AI-generated economic surplus is:

* **measured accurately**
* **distributed fairly**
* **protected from capture**
* **governed by multi-stakeholder legitimacy**
* **aligned with human capability, social stability, and democratic resilience**

The D-Project is **not** a tax, welfare, philanthropy, charity, or a redistribution mechanism.

It is a **co-investment return**:

> *If society co-creates the value, society receives a share of the value.*

---

# **1. Problem Statement**

AI is generating unprecedented economic surplus.
Without governance, this surplus becomes:

* unequally distributed
* privately captured
* politically destabilizing
* economically distortive
* socially corrosive
* legitimacy-fragile

Existing tools (taxation, UBI, robot levies) fail politically, economically, or structurally.

The D-Project introduces the necessary **institutional layer** to govern AI surplus.

---

# **2. Governance Spine**

The spine is composed of three firewalled components:

---

## **2.1 Coherence Governance Board (CGB)**

The CGB is the system‚Äôs central decision body.

**Key Properties:**

* Multi-stakeholder
* No actor > 40% control
* Staggered terms
* Transparent voting
* Published decisions
* Mandatory rotation
* Distributed authority

The CGB cannot be controlled by corporations, governments, philanthropies, or any single actor.

---

## **2.2 Audit & Integrity Office (AIO)**

Independent oversight with firewalled funding.

**Key Properties:**

* Random audit assignment
* Rotating auditor corps
* No private funding sources
* No influence from regulated firms
* Public audit summaries
* Conflict-of-interest prohibitions

The AIO ensures that surplus measurement and allocations cannot be manipulated.

---

## **2.3 Legal Compliance & Sanctions Framework (LCSF)**

The enforcement arm.

**Key Properties:**

* Statutory contribution requirement
* Non-regression clauses
* Automatic penalties for non-compliance
* Judicial appeal rights
* Enforcement modeled on pensions / employment insurance

# **3. Boundary Architecture**

Defines who participates.

---

## **3.1 Included**

* AI-intensive firms
* Firms generating measurable AI surplus
* Public-sector AI systems
* Multinationals operating within jurisdiction
* Companies using GPAI at scale

---

## **3.2 Excluded**

* Firms generating **no** AI surplus
* Micro-enterprises (<10 employees or <$250k revenue)
* War zones / collapsed states
* Non-participating countries
* Individuals (no personal component)

The boundary model ensures political fairness and shields small enterprises.

# **4. Allocation Architecture**

AI surplus moves through **five stages**:

1. Surplus measured
2. Surplus audited
3. Funds collected into NADA
   (*National AI Dividend Account*)
4. Funds allocated to *four protected channels*
5. Distributed via programs, institutions, and SME pathways

---

## **4.1 Four Protected Channels**

* **Human Capability**
* **Time & Wellbeing**
* **Civic & Cultural Infrastructure**
* **Structural Equity & Public Goods**

Each channel has legally protected use cases.

---

## **4.2 Strict Exclusions**

Funds **cannot** be used for:

* defense
* political campaigns
* philanthropic foundations
* general government budgets
* foreign aid

Ring-fencing prevents diversion or political weaponization.
